I am feeling very anxious. Self Care Tips

Anxiety

  • Set small daily goals, compete with yourself
  • Accept that you can’t control everything
  • Exercise! Exercising is a great way to take your mind off of things
  • Unplug. Leave all electronics alone for a few hours
  • Get strength from others - join support groups & surround yourself with friends
  • Limit alcohol and caffeine

I don't feel happy with myself. Self Care Tips

Depression

  • Try your best to keep connections with others and stay connected! (maintain a support system)
  • Push yourself to do things that you enjoy (even if it seems exhausting or impossible)
  • Try to focus on your health! Especially regarding sleeping and eating.
  • Practice breaking free from bad thinking habits (all-or-nothing thinking, overgeneralization, mental filter, etc.)
  • Always seek professional help if you find yourself in a bad mental state.
  • Don’t limit your treatment options!

I don't feel happy with my weight. Self Care Tips

Eating Disorders

Every 62 minutes, at least one person dies due to eating disorders. As a result, eating Disorders have the highest mortality rate out of all mental illnesses. We together have to fight this statistic.

  • You are not alone. There are support groups that want to help. The National Association of Anorexia Nervosa and Associated Disorders holds some and you can find one near you here. Or you can join an online one here.
  • You can read survivors' stories here.
  • Take everything one step at a time. Take it one day, one meal, or even one moment at a time.
  • Notice your eating disorder urges, and then practicing acting opposite to that urge.

I have trouble focusing in school. Self Care Tips

ADHD

  • Try to avoid feeling that you are unintelligent or lazy! You are capable of finding useful methods to help you overcome ADHD in your everyday life.
  • Try your best to develop neat habits or build some type of structure in your work/school life ( calendar, planner, make lists, force yourself to do what you need to do, etc.)
  • Take things one step at a time! (think before you speak, try to stay on task, work on prioritization, etc.)
  • Minimize any external distractions
  • Get up and stretch/move around in between breaks in order to avoid fidgeting or tapping
  • Keep searching for the right combination of treatments for you!
